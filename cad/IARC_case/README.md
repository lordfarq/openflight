M5 Bolts needed: 18
Front piece of glass size: 166mm width x 115mm length x 2mm thick
Back piece of glass size: 166mm width x 225mm length x 2mm thick

Instructions for OpenFlight Case Build
  
1. Start by printing (1x) ```outer_case_top.stl```. This part will house pretty much everything. ![](images/openflight_case_image_1.png)
2. Print (2x) ```OPS_mount``` parts. These secure onto the OPS243 sensor.![](images/openflight_case_image_2.png)
3. Once both mounts are secured to the OPS sensor, use (2x) M5 bolts to screw the entire OPS assembly into the larger left side of the outer_case_top part. Make sure the OPS assembly doesn’t protrude beyond the wall of the ```outer_case_top``` part. NOTE: I don’t know what screws are used for the OPS board itself…![](images/openflight_case_image_3.png)
4. Print (1x) ```KLD7_v_mount_L.stl``` and (1x) ```KLD7_v_mount_R.stl```. These will mount to one of the KLD7 sensors, and secure it in the vertical position.![](images/openflight_case_image_4.png)
5. Use (2x) M5 bolts to secure the vertical KLD7 assembly into the lower section on the right side of the ```outer_case_top``` part.![](images/openflight_case_image_5.png)
6. Print (1x) ```KLD7_h_mount_L.stl``` and (1x) ```KLD7_h_mount_R.stl```. These will mount to the other KLD7 sensor, and secure it in the horizontal position.![](images/openflight_case_image_6.png)
7. Use (2x) M5 bolts to secure the horizontal KLD7 assembly into the upper section on the right side of the ```outer_case_top part```.![](images/openflight_case_image_7.png)
8. Print out (1x) ```outer_case_bottom.stl``` ![](images/openflight_case_image_8.png)
9. Print out (4x) ```case_foot.stl``` parts.![](images/openflight_case_image_9.png)
10. Use (4x) M5 bolts to secure each ```case_foot``` part to the underside of the ```outer_case_bottom``` part. ![](images/openflight_case_image_10.png)
11. Use (4x) M5 bolts to secure the ```outer_case_bottom``` to the ```outer_case_top part```. ![](images/openflight_case_image_11.png)

And that’s how you put together the 3d printed parts!
